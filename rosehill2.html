Lot and Item Condition Definitions The following list details the criterion Rosehill Online uses to determine the condition of lots and items sold on our auction.

  • Brand New
    The item appears untouched although the packaging could be opened or seal broken by Rosehill Online to verify its condition. The packaging is the original packaging but may not be in perfect condition. No parts are missing and packing material is present. 
  • New Condition
    The item appears to have been opened and handled by another. Although the item has been removed from its packaging it has been returned in excellent condition. It differs from brand new in that it has been opened and handled previously. The packaging might not be the original and packing material may be missing.
  • Refurbished
    An item officially labelled as refurbished by the manufacturer. The item is in new condition and appears and operates as new.
  • New with Issue
    Items in this category are deemed new or brand new but have a flaw, packing damage or other issue caused by the manufacturer or from packing issues or transporting. The item did not receive the issue from a previous buyer. Please see comments for details.
  • Lightly Used
    This item has been inspected and tested. The item, to our best knowledge, functions as intended without issue. The product is in excellent condition but may have light wear marks or other signs of normal use. Lightly Used items are not new but are also not well used. 
  • Used
    Items listed as new are functioning and have been tested to the best of our ability. The item will have signs of varying use. Original packaging may not be present and manuals and assembly instructions may also not be included. This item appears to have been used for its purpose more than multiple times beyond the standards of lightly used.
  • Used with Issue
    An item that is used and functions but also has a condition that is not from normal wear and tear. The issue is not cosmetic but maybe significant to the buyer depending on the nature of the issue. Please see listers’ comments. 
  • Salvage
    Items deemed salvage are considered not functional as intended but may have value as parts or the be repurposed for another use. 
  • “As Is”
    An item that is unique in its condition. The issues will be noted by the lister and represented in the issue photos. Listings considered “As Is” are non returnable and it is up to the bidder to be satisfied with our listing or enquire if they have any questions prior to bidding. The option to preview “As Is” items can be arranged prior to the closing.
